Mathematics in Chemistry

A brief introduction to the many applications of mathematics in the field of Chemistry


Punnet Squares


Punnet squares are used to predict the chances of inheriting certain traits - for example, the likelihood of a flower being pink when it had one pink parent flower and one white parent flower.


A (dominant) a (recessive)
A (dominant) AA Aa
a (recessive) Aa aa

This is a diagram of a Punnet square showing the likelihood of inheriting a particular genotype of a certain gene, when the parents are both heterozygous - they have both a dominant and recessive allele. The capital A genotype is dominant, so we can see that there is a 3/4 , or 75% change that the offspring will inherit the dominant genotype.


a (recessive) a (recessive)
A (dominant) Aa Aa
a (recessive) aa aa

In this Punnet square, we can see that one parent is heterozygous and the other is homozygous. The probability of the offspring inheriting the dominant genotype is 50%.


Mathematical Models


Mathematics models are used frequently in various areas of biology to analyse and predict the behaviours of events. For example, mathematical modelling was used hugely during the COVID-19 pandemic to better aid decision making. It can also be used to examine the dynamics within a population - for example, how the removal of a certain food source would affect wildlife.


Other Uses


Many parts of maths can be used in biology. For example, calculus can be used to calculate the rate of change, for example in bacteria growth, or population growth. Statistics is also an important part of modelling processes, and not every process has one set outcome - several factors can affect what will happen. Additionally, biologists need to be confident using and calculating percentages, for example when investigating the effect of different substances on the mass of different tissues. Additionally, knowledge of inverse proportionality is required to understand the inverse square law for photosynthesis.
